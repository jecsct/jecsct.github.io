My thesis, "InsureConnect: Blockchain and Digital Identity in the Insurance Market", explored how blockchain and self-sovereign identity (SSI) technologies can transform the insurance sector ‚Äî particularly in streamlining the process of contract management and property damage claims.

The main goal of the project was to design and develop a decentralized registry platform where insurance companies and clients could interact securely, transparently, and efficiently ‚Äî without relying on traditional login systems. Instead, digital identities compliant with SSI principles were used for authentication and signing actions.

Key features of the platform included:

  • Smart contract-based workflows to register and manage insurance agreements and claims.
  • SSI-based identity verification to ensure data privacy and ownership by clients and insurers.
  • Blockchain integration (Hyperledger Fabric) for immutability and traceability of all actions.
  • Decentralized file storage using IPFS for handling large files like images or documents related to claims, while storing the content address (hash) on the blockchain.

The outcome was a working prototype that demonstrated how blockchain and SSI can enhance trust, security, and efficiency in real-world insurance interactions ‚Äî reducing fraud and minimizing administrative overhead.
